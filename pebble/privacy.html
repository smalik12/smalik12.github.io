Pebble Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 26, 2026

Pebble is a personal-finance app that runs on your device. It does not have a server, and it does not send your financial data to anyone.

What Pebble does NOT collect

Pebble does not collect, transmit, or store on any external server:

  • Your name, email, or contact details
  • Your financial transactions, balances, or income
  • Your credit card numbers (only optional last-four digits, stored on-device)
  • Any analytics, telemetry, or crash reports
  • Advertising identifiers

Pebble does not integrate with any third-party services, advertising networks, or analytics providers.

Where your data lives

Your data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework.

If you are signed into iCloud, your data syncs across your other devices through your private iCloud container, managed by Apple. Pebble does not have access to your iCloud account or your iCloud-stored data — only Apple does, under the privacy terms you accepted when you set up your iCloud account.

In-App Purchases

Pebble offers a one-time, non-consumable purchase ("Pebble Pro") through Apple's StoreKit. Apple handles all payment processing, receipt validation, and family sharing. Pebble itself does not receive your payment information.

Children

Pebble is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information about anyone — including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will be revised. The current version always lives at this URL.
